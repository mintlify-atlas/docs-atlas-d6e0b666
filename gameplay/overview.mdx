## The premise

You are trapped in a cursed casino. The only way out is to prove your memory before time runs out. Find all matching card pairs and you escape with your soul. Fail — and you lose it.

The atmosphere reinforces the stakes: a flickering red point light, exponential fog that closes in around you, a looping horror soundtrack, and a countdown timer labeled **VIDA** (Spanish for "life") that ticks down relentlessly the moment you touch the first card.

## The board

The game places **8 cards** on the round casino table in a **4×2 grid**. The cards are shuffled randomly at the start of every round.

There are **4 unique image pairs**:

| Pair | Image file |
|---|---|
| 1 | `img/img1.jpg` |
| 2 | `img/img2.jpg` |
| 3 | `img/img3.jpg` |
| 4 | `img/img4.jpg` |

All cards start face-down, showing the card back (`img/atras.jpeg`). The shuffle is performed with `Array.sort(() => Math.random() - 0.5)` each time the game initializes, so no two rounds have the same layout.

## The VIDA timer

You have **40 seconds** to find all 4 pairs.

<Note>
  The timer does **not** start when the page loads. It starts on your **first card hover**. Until you hover over a card, the counter stays at `VIDA: 40s` and no music plays. Use this moment to study the board before committing.
</Note>

The timer counts down in 1-second intervals. The current value is displayed in-world as `VIDA: Xs`, attached to the camera so it is always in view.

## How to play

<Steps>
  <Step title="Hover over a card to flip it">
    Move your cursor over any face-down card. A card-flip sound plays (`cartas.mp3`), the card rotates 180° around its Y-axis over 250ms, and the image is revealed at the halfway point of the animation (125ms in).

    If this is your first card interaction, the background music (`fondo.mp3`) starts and the 40-second countdown begins.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Hover over a second card">
    After flipping the first card, hover over a second face-down card. The same flip animation plays.

    Once two cards are face-up, the board locks — you cannot flip additional cards until the outcome of the current pair is resolved.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Match or mismatch">
    The game compares the `data-id` of both flipped cards:

    - **Match:** After 600ms, both cards shrink to scale `0 0 0` over 300ms and are removed from play. The pair counter increments.
    - **Mismatch:** After 800ms, both cards rotate back to `0 0 0` (face-down) over 250ms, and their `src` is restored to the card back at the 125ms midpoint. The board unlocks and you can flip again.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Repeat until all pairs are found or time runs out">
    Continue flipping pairs. You win by finding all 4 pairs. You lose if the timer reaches 0.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Win condition

When you find the **4th pair** (`pairsFound === 4`), the game:

1. Stops the timer.
2. Displays the message **SALISTE CON VIDA** ("You escaped with your life") in large red text in the 3D scene.
3. Automatically reloads the page after **4 seconds**, starting a new round.

## Lose condition

When the timer reaches **0**, the game:

1. Stops the timer.
2. Displays the message **PERDISTE TU ALMA** ("You lost your soul") in large red text.
3. Fades a dark red overlay (`#deathOverlay`) from transparent to fully opaque over 1 second, covering the entire view.
4. Automatically reloads the page after **3 seconds**.

## Board lock

During the resolution window (while the game is deciding match or mismatch), a `lockBoard` flag is set to `true`. Hovering over cards while the board is locked has no effect. The lock releases automatically once the outcome animation completes.

<Tip>
  Memorize card positions as they are revealed during mismatches. Cards flip back face-down but their positions do not change — a card you saw earlier will still be there.
</Tip>
